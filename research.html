Research Areas

Our work spans cognitive science, computational modelling, and AI.

Cognitive Modelling

We build computational models that describe how humans make decisions under uncertainty and with incomplete information. Using Bayesian inference and reinforcement learning frameworks, we compare model predictions with behavioural data to infer the computational principles underlying the brain.

Emotion & Wellbeing

This theme explores the behavioural and neural mechanisms of emotion regulation. Using wearable devices and smartphone apps, we collect emotion data from everyday life and develop digital interventions that can support users' mental wellbeing in real time.

Human–AI Interaction

As AI enters daily life, we study how people understand, trust, and collaborate with AI systems, and how these interactions affect learning outcomes and cognitive load — with the goal of designing more human-centred AI.

Learning & Memory

Combining behavioural experiments with computational models, we study how humans form, consolidate, and retrieve memories, and how strategies such as spaced learning can improve long-term retention.
