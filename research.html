Research

Controlling light in multimode fibres — from fundamental nonlinear optics to real-world photonic devices.

Nonlinear Optics in Multimode Fibres

Stimulated Brillouin scattering (SBS) and transverse mode instability (TMI) limit how much power a fibre can carry. We study the physics of these nonlinearities in multimode fibres and develop strategies — including multimode excitation and wavefront control — to suppress them and reach higher powers.

Heat-Free (Radiation-Balanced) Fibre Lasers

By exploiting light-induced anti-Stokes fluorescence, Yb-doped silica fibres can be optically cooled. We use this radiation-balanced cooling to build heat-free fibre lasers and amplifiers, and to develop high-precision fibre-optic sensors for measuring their temperature.

Wavefront Shaping & Mode Control

The spatial modes inside a fibre can be programmed. We use wavefront shaping and optimisation to control the output beam profile, focus light through complex fibres, and steer optical nonlinearities to our advantage.

Programmable & Chiral Photonic Devices

Chiral liquid-crystalline photonic crystals provide a soft, tunable platform for manipulating polarisation, modulating ultrafast pulses, and building reconfigurable photonic devices.

High-Precision Fibre-Optic Sensing

Slow-light fibre-Bragg-grating sensors enable temperature and strain measurements with very high resolution — essential tools both for our laser-cooling experiments and for wider sensing applications.

Applications

The techniques we develop reach across many fields, from directed energy and lidar to quantum information, AI, and space photonics.
